# Design Rationale – Evening Reset Reflection Tree

## Why I Chose These Questions

I designed questions that feel natural for a tired employee at the end of the day.  
The goal was to create a reflection conversation, not a survey.

Each question focuses on real workplace moments:

- How the day felt
- How the employee responded to difficulty
- Whether they contributed or expected recognition
- Whether they thought only about themselves or also others

These moments are easier to answer honestly than abstract psychology terms.

---

## How I Designed the Branching

The tree follows three axes in sequence:

1. **Agency**  
First, the user reflects on whether they acted with control or felt controlled by events.

2. **Contribution**  
After agency, the user shifts toward what they gave rather than what they expected.

3. **Impact Radius**  
Finally, the user considers who was affected beyond themselves.

This sequence creates a natural widening perspective.

---

## Trade-offs I Made

I chose clarity over complexity.

Instead of many confusing branches, I used clean branching with readable options.  
This keeps the experience short, usable, and deterministic.

I also avoided judgmental wording.  
The tool should guide awareness, not shame the user.

## Psychological Sources

The design was inspired by:

- Julian Rotter – Locus of Control

- Perspective-Taking psychology
